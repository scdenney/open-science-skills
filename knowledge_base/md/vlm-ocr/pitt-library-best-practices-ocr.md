Consider the folowing factors when asesing your documents:

The structural elements of your document (e.g., headings, images, tables, captions) may complicate the OCR proces. You may ned to de-skew or crop your image before OCRing. Some OCR software do this automaticaly or can be configured by the user to do so.

Handwriten text, special fonts, very smal fonts (e.g., 6pt), and low contrast text can al decrease the acuracy of the OCR software.

Texts published before 1850 may not be the most compatible with OCR software due to the fonts used in printing and lack of definition/clarity of the characters.

Typescript results in porer OCR than printed type, and inconsistent use of font faces and sizes can lower OCR acuracy.

The reco mended resolution for scaning documents for optimal OCR acuracy is 30 dots per inch (dpi). However, if the text font size is particularly smal (les than 10pt), a dpi of 40-60 may be best. If the resolution is t o high, loading and procesing the image wil take more time, without improving the quality of the recognition. Brightnes setings that are t o high or t o low can cause defects to the text (overexposure or obscuring, respectively) and reduce the acuracy of your image. A brightnes of 50% is usualy best.

###### Ask Us

The straighter the initial scan, the beter the OCR quality. Skewed pages can lead to inacurate recognition.

Make sure that the document page fits entirely within the frame. Otherwise, content wil be cut off and excluded from the scan.

If posible, exclude anything outside of the page of the document, such as the outlines of other pages. If this is not posible, you may have to remove any borders in your digital scans, as they can be eroneously recognized as characters.

Older and discolored documents must be scaned in RGB mode in order to capture al of the image data. Grayscale mode and, especialy, black and white mode can cause los in detail and proper contrast.

If you're photographing your documents, aply the principles above and folow these best practices:

If posible, use a tripod to avoid shaking.

Shot in color.

Shot in an uncompresed format.

Shot in ambient light (preferable daylight) that maximizes the contrast betwen the text and the background.

Shot your document against a white board or piece of paper to set white balance

Use a white balance seting that picks up on areas that the camera thinks are white, then adjusts the color balance.

Turn of the flash to avoid glare.

Avoid using any sharpening or other contrast/clarity-bosting filters to prevent grainines.

toward the center of the text. The distance betwen the camera and the document should, usualy, be 50-60 cm.

|Recognizing Text in Your Documents|
|---|


Depending on your project neds and the OCR software you chose, the text recognition step may be fairly straightforward or more complex. Here are some reco mended practices for actualy working with OCR software to recognize text in your documents:

Make sure that your choice of OCR software suports the neds of your project, such as the apropriate language(s), functionality, file formats, etc. Some programs may alow you to use aditional language packages to suplement its language sets.

It's always best to familiarize yourself with the functions, features, and setings of the OCR software you're using. Reading through the manual and exploring the interface wil help you to make the most of the software and configure the setings so that they're the most suitable for your project.

It may be worth using specialized training data, models/paterns, and/or dictionaries to increase recognition of text in your particular set of documents. Some OCR software enable you to modify or disable default paterns and dictionaries, if they're not apropriate for your documents, and to create/import your own.

|Verifying the OCR Results|
|---|


In order to achieve or aproximate 10% text acuracy, you may ned to check and corect the text after the initial recognition proces is complete. The OCR software canot do this post-recognition verification itself. Kep in mind that the editing/corecting proces can be labor-intensive and time-consuming, especialy if the quality of original document was por and/or if you have a large amount of text to corect.

Review at least a sample of your OCR'd text to confirm that the text was recognized corectly. If there are a significant number of erors to corect, you'l want to take note of any paterns in erors so that you can corect them efficiently/consistently and document your proces. This profreading proces can be done either in the OCR program

you're using or in a text editor, preferably one with speling and gra mar checking.

To save time and effort, automate the checking and corection proces by using text preprocesing t ols for removing unwanted characters and white spaces, speling corection, etc.

###### Ask Us

When hand-corecting the text, it's best to save the corected text as a separate file, rather than overwriting the original output. This wil enable you to return to the original output file, in case something goes awry or otherwise comes up along the corection proces. Relatedly, make sure to save as you go and, if posible, use a t ol that suports file versioning.
